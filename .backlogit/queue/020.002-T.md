- `process_pdf_triaged(path, *, output_dir, baseline_engine='markitdown', ...)` and `triage_report_only(path, *, output_dir, report_tsv_path, baseline_engine='markitdown', ...)` both accept the new kwarg.
- Default behavior with no kwarg routes through markitdown.
- baseline_engine='pypdf' restores the 021-S/PA3 behavior bit-for-bit (regression coverage for the pypdf path).
- TriageResult.metadata['baseline_engine'] is recorded for every run.
- markitdown failure on a page falls back to pypdf for that page and increments TriageResult.metadata['baseline_engine_fallback'] (defensive — markitdown could fail on weird PDFs).
- dispatch_pdf_mode('triage', path, output_dir=..., baseline_engine='pypdf') forwards the kwarg correctly.

<!-- BEGIN:description -->
Add a `baseline_engine: Literal['markitdown', 'pypdf'] = 'markitdown'` kwarg to `process_pdf_triaged` and `triage_report_only`. Introduce a `_heuristic_extract(reader, page_idx, engine, splice_cache) -> str` helper that dispatches on engine choice. For `markitdown`, splice the single page to a temp PDF (reuse the existing splice cache directory) and invoke `MarkItDown(enable_plugins=False).convert(splice).text_content`. For `pypdf`, current `reader.pages[page_idx].extract_text() or ''`.

Wire `dispatch_pdf_mode` to forward `baseline_engine` from `**kwargs`. Record `baseline_engine` AND `baseline_engine_fallback` in `TriageResult.metadata`.

Depends on: U4 (markitdown must be installed first).

- tests/process/test_pdf_triage.py (modified — add baseline-engine comparison test)
