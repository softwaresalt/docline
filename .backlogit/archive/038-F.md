Fix the batched docling worker OCR OOM crash (access violation 0xC0000005) observed during the 036.002-T cosmos sweep (mg1, 2026-06-27). A batched group crashed while RapidOCR ran (std::bad_alloc on the onnxruntime Clip node, numpy _ArrayMemoryError), and the whole group fell back to heuristic (subprocess_fallback_count=1), contaminating wall-clock.

Root cause (grounded in code):
- Concern (1) "conditional-OCR gating not applied on the batched path" is REFUTED. Both batched paths already forward per-range/per-chunk do_ocr (pdf_triage.py:570 via _range_needs_ocr; pdf_batch.py:284 via _chunk_needs_ocr), and 036-S tests pin it (test_conditional_ocr.py). page_needs_ocr flags a page only when text <200 chars AND it has embedded images, so a native-text Azure page that is mostly a screenshot with a short caption legitimately trips the gate.
- Concern (2) is the REAL defect: group_by_page_count (MAX_BATCHED_PAGES=40) bounds page count, not OCR/bitmap memory, and a hard OCR bad_alloc kills the entire worker subprocess so EVERY range/chunk in that group falls back to heuristic. One OCR page drags down up to 40 pages of OCR-free docling work.

Chosen fix direction: make batched grouping OCR-aware so OCR-flagged ranges/chunks are isolated under a tighter per-group cap. This bounds per-group bitmap memory (OCR is what loads bitmaps) and limits an OCR OOM blast radius to OCR work only, leaving OCR-free ranges in their own group to survive. Rejected: per-page DPI/downscale in the worker (touches docling internals, riskier); relying on serialize_docling (only triggers on low-RAM hosts; the OOM occurred on an ample-RAM box where batched mode was active).
